Landfill Site 2

In 2018, the landfill was completed on the site. Environmental pollution in the surrounding area was minimized through thorough follow-up management, including landfill cover surface, leachate, landfill gas, and separation section management.
We are discussing plans to use Landfill Site 2, where the landfill is finished, and looking at various possibilities, including establishing sports facilities, such as a swimming pool, cultural facilities, and new/renewable energy facilities.

Landfill area : 2,620,000 m2

Landfill Site 3-1

Landfill Site 3-1 began landfill operations in 2018. It is the first landfill site in Korea to use “separate landfilling.”
Separate landfilling is an advanced method that divides a landfill site into two, one for household waste and the other for construction waste. It allows flexible handling of wastes according to their characteristics and amount and minimizes the emissions of odor sources such as hydrogen sulfide, thereby improving air quality.

Landfill Site 3 is equipped with a landfill gas collection facility to collect landfill gas, which may cause odor, to prevent the gas from spreading into the atmosphere.

Site area : 1,033,000m2

Landfill area : 8,300,000m2

Landfill period : 2018. 09 ~

Landfill capacity : 18,190,000tons

Resource Circulation Town

It is a complex of waste-to-resources facilities to manage the facilities efficiently in one place.
Sludge recycling facility, metropolitan food waste effluent-to-biogas facility, and pilot facility to produce resources using combustible waste are in the town. It converts waste into resources and energy to create sustainable values for the future.
Currently, about 25 % of wastes coming to the Sudokwon Landfill Site are reborn as resources. We are aiming to raise the percentage to 77 %.

Main facilities

  • Sludge recycling facility (phase 1, 2, and 3)

  • Metropolitan food waste effluent-to-biogas facility

  • Pilot facility to produce resources using combustible waste

Integrated Weighbridge

Waste vehicles first pass through this facility to enter the Sudokwon Landfill Site. The facility identifies the types, origins, and amount of received waste in real-time.
It automatically recognizes waste vehicles and samples waste for inspection and analysis to prevent illegal waste from coming to the landfill site.

Areas from which receiving is allowed :
64 cities, gun, and gu in Seoul, Incheon, Gyeonggi, etc.

Average daily receiving amount :
About 12,000 tons (2020)

Leachate Treatment Facility

It is a facility with a leachate treatment capacity of 6,700 m3 per day. It treats leachate and food waste effluent in Landfill Sites 1, 2, and 3 and wastewater in Waste Energy Town together.
The water is biologically treated (stage 1 : anaerobic digestion tank, stage 2 : denitrification/nitrification tank), and then pollutants not degraded are chemically treated (stage 1: chemical coagulation tank, stage 2 : oxidation coagulation tank).

Treatment capacity : 6,700t/day

Construction expense : 66,800,000,000 won

Treatment target :
29 items including BOD, COD, and nitrogen

LFG Power Plant

It is the largest landfill gas (LFG) power plant in the world. It uses gases created in landfill sites to generate electricity.
Mechanical energy is converted into electric energy to produce electricity by the process of collecting and transporting landfill gas → removing hydrogen sulfide at a pre-processing facility → combusting in a boiler → producing high-temperature and high-pressure steam → and rolling turbine by steam.

Power plant installation period : 2004.3 ~ 2006.12

Electricity production :
About 300 million kWh per year (2020)

Electricity sales :
About 30 billion won per year (2020)

Dream Park CC

It is a 36-hole public golf course built on Landfill Site 1 buried with 65 million tons of waste for about nine years (1992-2000).
This eco-friendly place was built without forest destruction where agricultural chemicals are not used.
It hosted the 2014 Incheon AG Golf Games, 2016 KLPGA Games, 2017 KPGA Games, 2018 PGA Tour Qualifying Tournament, etc. It has been loved as a resting place for local residents as well.

Site area : 4,090,000 m2

Landfill area : 2,510,000 m2

Landfill period : 1992.02-2000.10

Landfill capacity : 64,250,000tons

Wildflower Garden

The SLC and local residents built the Wildflower Garden in an area that used to be a coal briquette ash yard.
It is a beloved walking and resting place. In the garden, colorful flowerbeds are considered photographic attractions.
Sculptures made of trees damaged by the typhoon and a long tunnel made of vines attract attention.
Every spring, cherry blossoms in full bloom are spectacular. The Metasequoia Road, which leads to the Wildflower Garden, is a popular shooting location for TV series, movies, and TV commercials.
The SLC employs local residents every year to manage the Wildflower Garden, contributing to job creation, environment improvement, and the residents’ income increase.

Area : 956,781m2
